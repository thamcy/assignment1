Marang Trail

Click here to return to the highlights

The beginning of the Southern Ridges, Marang Trail is the first thing you see when you step off the escalator exiting Harbourfront MRT. If you are lucky, you might spot the Greater Racket-tailed Drongo (Dicrurus paradiseus) and other birds amid the lush greenery.

Note: The trail is not lit after dark. Please bring a torch and exercise caution if you wish to explore the trail at night.

Faber Walk

Click here to return to the highlights

At the top of Marang Trail, the mostly sheltered Faber Walk takes you on a loop around the peak of Mount Faber. Follow the paved walkways to enjoy spectacular views of the southern part of Singpaore and the Southern Islands. At night, the twinkling lights of the distant city mesmerises observers.

Note: There are F&B establishments at Faber Park where you can chill out with a beer or ice-cream while enjoying the view.

Henderson Waves

Click here to return to the highlights

At the start of Henderson Waves stands a nutmeg tree - a symbol of this area's past when it was a nutmeg plantation. Walk a little further and you will see the distinctive wave-like structure of the 274m-long pedestrian bridge that matches it's name perfectly.
This charming, wood-panelled bridge was built to improve connectivity between nature areas. A series of seven undulating "ribs" double up as alcoves and provide shelter. Between 7pm to 7am, the bridge takes on a different look, with the wave-form lit up by attractive LED lights.
Birdwatchers visiting between September and April get to spot various birds, such as the Brahminy Kite (Haliastur indus), raptors and other winter visitors, from atop the bridge. Take a quick breather at the old guard house and hydrate yourself before carrying on with the journey.

Note: The Henderson Waves is a popular spot for couples, wedding photos, instagrammers and many more. It might be very crowded when you go so be prepared!

Sembcorp Forest of Giants

Click here to return to the highlights

Past the Henderson Waves and on the right is Sembcorp Forest of Giants, a living gallery og giant tree species that are threatened in the region. Sponsored by Sembcorp via NParks' registered charity and IPC, Garden City Fund, the forest has a collection of more than 600 trees that were planted to educate people about these towering species and raise awareness for reforestation.
The restroom next to Sembcorp Forest of Giants is a good place to take a break before you continue to the next attraction.

Note: Look out for Tualang (Koompassia excelsa), Kempas (Koompassia malaccensis) and Jelutong (Dyera costulata) as shown on the left in order.

Alkaff Mansion

Click here to return to the highlights

A hundred metres from Sembcorp Forest of Giants lies the white and orange Alkaff Mansion at Telok Blangah Green. This unique conservation mansion restored by the Singapore Tourism Board serves delectable cuisine to visitors drawn to its historic charm.
A panoramic view of Singapore can be enjoyed from the semicircular terraces of the Terrace Garden, which is the vantage point of Telok Blangah hill Park.

Note: Alkaff Mansion was built primarily as a retreat for the Alkaff family and to entertain their numerous clients and guests. Their American guests would sometimes refer to the hill as Mount Washington.

Forest Walk

Click here to return to the highlights

Take a stroll on the step-free elevated Forest Walk of Telok Blangah Hill Park. Enjoy 1.3km of lush secondary forest canopy before moving on to Alexandra Arch.
The elevated walkway is an excellent platform for spotting birds many different species of birds. The ones shown on the left are in the following order: Pink-necked Green Pigeon (Treron vernans), Straw-headed Bulbul (Pycnonotus zeylanicus), Common Iora (Aegithina tiphia) and Brown-throated Sunbird (Anthreptes malacensis).

Note: You should also be able to spot Simpoh Air (Dillenia indica), a large common shrub used in the past to wrap food items.

The Singing Forest

Click here to return to the highlights

Midway through Forest Walk, you will find the Singing Forest, an area with more than 200 bird-attracting trees native to the region.
While there is no guarantee that any particular bird species will lend its voice to the Singing Forest, NParks' tree planning ensures that food sources for them are increased so as to attract a larger number and wider variety of birds.

Note: The commonly spotted birds here include the Common Tailorbird (Orthotomus sutorius), White-throated Kingfisher (Halcyon smyrnensis) and Dollarbird (Eurystomus orientalis). They are shown in order on the left.

Alexandra Arch

Click here to return to the highlights

Alexandra Arch links Forest Walk to Floral Walk at HortPark. This 80m-long bridge opens like a leaf over Alexandra Road. In the evening, attractive colour-changing LED lights on the bridge complement the dusk falling over the forested hill parks. It is a dazzling sight you do not want to miss!

HortPark

Click here to return to the highlights

Be greeted by a floral runway at HortPark - a unique park that brings together nature appreciation and gardening-related activities, be it recreational, educational, research or retail.
Families and trekkers can relax by the Water Garden while children run freely on HortLawn, a spacious plot next to the Greenhouse. The Greenhouse-Garden Retail area offers gardening items for sale or you can simply grab a quick snack and cool down with a drink.
Take two hours to explore the rest of this 23-hectare park, which features a collection of beautiful themed gardens suitable for indoor and outdoor activities. Next to the Vineyard, an alfresco fine dining restaurant, is the Lifestyle Corner, an area where you can find a range of indoor plants and DIY decor ideas for your home or office.
Walk down the boardwalk and enjoy the various themed gardens that showcase to home owners different concepts and gardening trends. Children will also gain a better appreciation of plants and nature.

Note: The different gardens seen on the left are just some of the many found at HortPark. Drop by HortPark to explore more!

Canopy Walk

Click here to return to the highlights

Past the Prototype Glasshouses is a park connector that links HortPark to Kent Ridge Park. The Canopy Walk can be reached via this park connector.
Canopy Walk is yet another trail on the Southern Ridges that offers trekkers the experience of walking through a secondary forest at eye-level with the forest canopy. It takes about 10 minutes to explore this 280m-long boardwalk that links Kent Ridge Park to the museum “Reflections at Bukit Chandu”.
Listen out for the sounds of birds and insects interrupting the serenity of the park. Sunbirds, doves, squirrels, lizards and the White-crested Laughingthrush (Garrulax leucolophus) are part of the rich biodiversity thriving here.
One of the last battles of Singapore was fought on this very hill, which overlooks the harbour and the British military depots in the Ayer Rajah area.
Reflections at Bukit Chandu Museum houses the history of Opium Hill and the Malay Regiment that defended the Pasir Panjang area in World War II.

Note: This is the end of this trail and the following are ways to get to the nearest MRT.

  • Walk down Pepys Road to Pasir Panjang MRT Station
  • Walk to end of Science Park Drive. Take bus service 92 to Kent Ridge MRT Station
  • Walk down Vigilante Drive. Take bus service 200 to Haw Par Villa MRT Station
